What is BMR?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions while at rest — like breathing, circulation, and cell production.

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, the most accurate formula available:

  • Male: BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5
  • Female: BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161
  • W = weight (kg), H = height (cm), A = age (years)

What is TDEE?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories you burn per day when exercise and physical activity are taken into account.

TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier

  • This is your maintenance calorie level
  • Eat less to lose weight (deficit of 500 cal/day = ~1 lb/week loss)
  • Eat more to gain weight (surplus of 300-500 cal/day)

Macronutrients Guide

  • Protein: 4 cal/g — builds muscle, keeps you full
  • Carbohydrates: 4 cal/g — primary energy source
  • Fat: 9 cal/g — hormones, brain health
  • For weight loss: High protein (35%), moderate carbs (35%), fat (30%)
  • For muscle gain: High protein (30%), high carbs (45%), fat (25%)
  • Maintenance: Protein (25%), carbs (50%), fat (25%)
